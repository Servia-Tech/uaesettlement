UAE Gratuity Law Explained: Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021

The exact formula, the 2-year cap, and what changed for anyone who resigns.

The law behind every UAE gratuity calculation

Every gratuity figure on this site — and every gratuity payment made by a mainland UAE employer — is governed by one law: Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021 on the Regulation of Labour Relations, which came into effect on 2 February 2022. It replaced the older Federal Law No. 8 of 1980 and modernised several parts of UAE employment law, including how end-of-service gratuity is worked out.

The gratuity formula, in plain English

If you have completed at least 1 year of continuous service, you are entitled to gratuity calculated as:

Years of serviceGratuity per year
First 5 years21 days of basic salary, per year
Every year beyond 530 days of basic salary, per year

Your daily wage for this calculation is your basic monthly salary ÷ 30 — allowances like housing, transport or commission are not included. The total is capped at 2 years' worth of basic wage, however long you actually worked.

What actually changed in 2021 — and why it matters if you resign

The most significant practical change for most people is this: under the old 1980 law, employees on unlimited contracts who resigned (rather than being terminated) had their gratuity cut on a sliding scale — as little as one-third of the full amount after 1-3 years of service, two-thirds after 3-5 years, and full gratuity only after 5+ years.

Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021 removed that reduction entirely. Today, whether you resign or are terminated makes no difference to your gratuity amount, as long as you completed at least 1 year of continuous service. The only thing that still matters is your length of service and your basic salary.

What the law does not cover

  • Free zones such as DIFC and ADGM run their own separate employment regulations, not Federal Decree-Law 33/2021.
  • Domestic workers (maids, drivers, nannies) are covered by a separate law, Federal Law No. 9 of 2022.
  • Less than 1 year of continuous service means no gratuity is owed under the law, regardless of how the job ended.

Frequently asked questions

What is UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021?

It is the current UAE labour law that took effect on 2 February 2022, replacing the older Federal Law No. 8 of 1980. It governs mainland UAE employment contracts, including end-of-service gratuity, notice periods, working hours and leave.

Do I still get gratuity if I resign in the UAE?

Yes. Under Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021, resignation no longer reduces your gratuity. Anyone with more than 1 year of continuous service receives the full 21/30-day calculation, regardless of whether they resigned or were terminated.

Is there a maximum amount of gratuity I can receive?

Yes, gratuity is capped at 2 years' worth of basic wage, no matter how many years you worked beyond that point.

What was different under the old UAE labour law before 2022?

Under the old Federal Law No. 8 of 1980, employees on unlimited contracts who resigned (rather than were terminated) had their gratuity reduced on a sliding scale — as little as one-third after 1-3 years of service. That reduction was removed entirely under the 2021 law.
